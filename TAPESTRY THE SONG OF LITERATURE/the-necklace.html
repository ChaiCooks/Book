The Necklace

by Guy de Maupassant

üìñ Summary

Mathilde Loisel, a young woman of beauty and charm, feels trapped in her modest life married to a clerk. When her husband secures an invitation to a prestigious ball, Mathilde borrows a diamond necklace from her wealthy friend Madame Forestier. At the ball, she is the center of attention, but upon returning home, she discovers the necklace is lost. The Loisels replace it with an identical one, plunging themselves into ten years of grueling debt and poverty. Only after Mathilde has aged beyond her years does she learn the devastating truth: the original necklace was merely an imitation, worth almost nothing.

üéØ Key Themes

  • The Deceptiveness of Appearances The necklace appears valuable but is fake; Mathilde appears wealthy at the ball but is not.
  • The Cost of Pride and Vanity Mathilde's desire for social status leads to a decade of suffering and lost youth.
  • Social Class and Ambition The story critiques the rigid class structures of 19th-century French society.
  • Irony and Fate The cruel twist of fate reveals how unnecessary their suffering was.
